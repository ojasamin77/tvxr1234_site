The Problem

Three things draining your practice. All of them relational.

01

Guidance hours your practice never recovers

Family guidance is among the highest-value billable services in ABA. It is also among the most underdelivered. Not because BCBAs don't know what families need clinically — but because the conversations required to keep families engaged, aligned, and showing up are emotionally demanding in ways clinical training does not address. When a parent cancels, disengages, or loses confidence in the plan, that authorized hour disappears. Across a mid-size practice, those hours add up to a revenue gap that compounds every month.

02

Staff who leave before they hit their stride

The majority of BCBAs entering practice today are under 35 with fewer than five years of experience. Practices hope they learn relational skills on the job. Most don't — not because they lack the capability, but because the job gives them no safe place to practice before the stakes are real. The hard conversations happen with actual families. When those conversations go badly often enough, burnout follows. And then they leave.

03

Feedback that doesn't change behavior

Corrective feedback that is too vague gets ignored. Feedback that is too blunt damages the relationship and accelerates turnover. Most supervisors default to one or the other — not because they don't care, but because they were never taught how to calibrate. Protocol drift continues. The clinical standard you built your practice around erodes one avoided conversation at a time.

The Revenue Reality

This is not a culture problem. It is a revenue problem.

A mid-size ABA practice with 100 active clients delivering half the authorized guidance hours it could be delivering is leaving between $288,000 and $384,000 on the table every year. That number does not include the cost of replacing a BCBA — typically $25,000–$45,000 per departure when recruiting, onboarding, and lost productivity are factored in. The relational skill gap is not soft. It has a dollar figure.

How It Works

You deploy it. Your BCBAs use it. Your numbers move.

01

Your BCBAs practice in private

Each BCBA accesses realistic, ABA-specific conversation scenarios — parent pushback, corrective supervision, organizational pressure. They respond in their own words. No colleagues watching. No performance review attached. The private environment produces more authentic behavioral signal and higher voluntary engagement than any top-down training mandate.

02

Behavioral feedback after every session

After each conversation, the platform surfaces specific, observable feedback. What the BCBA said that moved the conversation forward. What escalated it. What to try differently next time. Grounded in ABA measurement principles. Built for clinical staff, not generic corporate audiences.

03

Your practice sees the operational result

Better parent conversations mean fewer cancellations and stronger guidance utilization. Clearer supervision conversations mean less protocol drift and more consistent data. Staff who feel supported in the hardest parts of their job stay longer.

Why Transform

Every other solution teaches theory. This one changes behavior.

Passive CEU content does not change how a BCBA handles a defensive parent at 4pm on a Thursday. Repeated practice under realistic conditions does. Transform is built on the same behavioral principles BCBAs apply to their clients — applied to the BCBAs themselves.

Supervision does not scale. Every hour a supervisor spends coaching conversation skills is an hour not spent on clinical oversight. Transform gives your staff unlimited private practice without touching your supervision bandwidth.
