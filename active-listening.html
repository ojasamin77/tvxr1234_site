Real Supervisory Moments

Step into three scenarios that BCBAs navigate every week

Each scenario adapts in real time to how you communicate. The situation is real. The stakes are safe.

Scenario 01

Navigating a sensitive conversation with a parent about their child's treatment progress

The data tells one story. The parent's experience tells another. Practice staying present, validating the relationship, and holding the clinical plan at the same time.

Scenario 02

Coaching an RBT who is struggling with timeliness and attendance

A staff member who matters to the team is starting to slip. Practice addressing the pattern directly without making it personal or losing the relationship.

Scenario 03

Receiving a difficult performance review from a supervisor

Feedback that catches you off guard. Practice staying regulated, asking the right questions, and leaving the conversation with clarity rather than defensiveness.

Learning Objectives

What you will be able to do by the end

Objectives are behavioral and observable, not theoretical. You will know you have met them because your responses in the scenarios will change.
