The RIQ Framework

What relational intelligence actually measures

The assessment is built on five domains of Relationship Intelligence, each covering the observable behaviors that determine how people relate under pressure.

Awareness and Recognition

  • Reflective self-insight
  • Perspective-taking
  • Sociocultural awareness and adaptation
  • Emotional recognition
  • Balanced disclosure
  • Solution awareness

Communication and Listening

  • Respectful listening
  • Solution-oriented thinking
  • Neutral and nonjudgmental language
  • Clarity and conciseness
  • Curiosity
  • Present and attentive

Relational Integrity and Trust

  • Establishing a relationship
  • Accountability
  • Consistency and dependability
  • Respecting boundaries
  • Psychological safety
  • Thoughtful deliberation

Emotional Regulation and Responsiveness

  • Self-regulation of emotions
  • Empathy
  • Flexibility
  • De-escalation skills
  • Vulnerability
  • Hopeful outlook

Relational Impact and Guidance

  • Mediation and negotiation skill
  • Collaboration and teaming
  • Moral and fair reasoning
  • Strategic thinking and problem solving
  • Risk assessment and mitigation
  • Empowering influence
What Changes

From guessing to knowing

Organizations that start with assessment move from ad-hoc training decisions to targeted development. They know which skills matter most for their specific context and where the real gaps are before spending resources on development.

  • Identify relational strengths and growth areas at the individual, team, and organizational level
  • Target coaching and role-play experiences to real relational needs, not assumptions
  • Monitor and measure progress over time
  • Connect relational dynamics to business outcomes leadership can act on

For individuals

A clear, private picture of relational strengths and the specific areas that most affect how you show up in high-stakes conversations.

For teams

A shared view of where trust and communication are working and where interpersonal patterns are creating friction or risk.

For organizations

The data needed to distinguish between individual skill gaps and systemic relational breakdowns, and to prioritize accordingly.
